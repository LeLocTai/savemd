The value of α that gives the lowest possible discrepancy is achieved if  α = 1 / ϕ , where ϕ is the golden ratio. That is,

ϕ ≡ 5 + 1 2 ≃ 1.61803398875 … ;
It is interesting to note that there are an infinite number of other values of  α that also achieve optimal discrepancy, and they are all related via the Moebius transformation
α ′ = p α + q r α + s for all integers p , q , r , s such that | p s − q r | = 1
We now compare this recurrence method to the well known van der Corput reverse-radix sequences [van der Corput, 1935] . The van der Corput sequences are actually a family of sequences, each defined by a unique hyper-parameter, b. The first few terms of the sequence for b=2 are:
t n [ 2 ] = 1 2 , 1 4 , 3 4 , 1 8 , 5 8 , 3 8 , 7 8 , 1 16 , 9 16 , 5 16 , 13 16 , 3 16 , 11 16 , 7 16 , 15 16 , …
